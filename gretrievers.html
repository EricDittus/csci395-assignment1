Golden Retrievers

Golden retrievers originated in Scotland. They were cross-bred by Sir Dudley Majoribanks in the 19th century. He used flat-coated retrievers and tweed water spaniels (along with other species). Golden retrievers are a medium-sized breed of dog and they are one of the most kept pets in the Western countries. They are generally very friendly and calm, making them easy to train. Their average lifespan is of 12-13 years. Their friendly and calm nature makes them a popular pet among families with kids, but who wouldn't like them regardless of age!
